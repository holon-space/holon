## Investigating Milk-Glass Backdrop Blur Support in GPUI

Explored whether GPUI can achieve the frosted glass effect seen in a design mockup, which requires per-element backdrop blur.

**What happened**

- Reviewed GPUI's built-in blur and transparency capabilities
- Identified window-level blur support via `WindowBackgroundAppearance::Blurred` (using `NSVisualEffectView` on macOS)
- Confirmed box shadow blur radius is available but doesn't affect backdrop
- Discovered per-element opacity is supported but insufficient for the desired effect
- Determined that **per-element backdrop blur is not currently supported** in GPUI

**Challenges**

- The mockup requires `backdrop-filter: blur()` semantics (blurring content *behind* specific elements), but GPUI only implements window-level blur (blurring the desktop wallpaper behind the entire window)
- This limitation exists because implementing per-element backdrop blur would require platform-specific infrastructure (like per-element `NSVisualEffectView` on macOS) that GPUI hasn't abstracted yet

**Workarounds identified**

- **Whole-window frosted glass**: Use `WindowBackgroundAppearance::Blurred` with semi-transparent panel backgrounds—works well for single overlays but can't blur internal content
- **Visual approximation**: Combine semi-transparent backgrounds (`hsla(0, 0, 1.0, 0.15)`) with soft shadows to achieve ~70% of the effect without actual blur
- **Fork and extend**: Add per-element `backdrop_filter` support to GPUI by implementing platform-specific visual effect views

**Status**: Documented the gap and provided three escalating workaround options; window-level blur or fake-blur approaches recommended for near-term use.

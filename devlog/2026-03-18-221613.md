## Refactor TestEnvironment to be a proper DI citizen

**Context:** TestEnvironment was storing a redundant `E2ETestContext` wrapper and constructing `FrontendSession` via `from_engine()` instead of from the DI provider, preventing sessions from accessing registered services like `LoroBlockOperations` and `PublishErrorTracker`.

**What happened:**

- Made `FrontendSession::from_provider()` public in `holon-frontend/src/lib.rs`
- Extracted `make_test_config()` helper in `test_environment.rs` to eliminate duplicated `HolonConfig` and `SessionConfig` construction (was inline in both `build_with()` and `start_app_with()`)
- Replaced `FrontendSession::from_engine(engine)` with `FrontendSession::from_provider(provider.clone(), &config_dir)` so sessions now own the real DI provider with all registered services
- Removed `ctx: Option<E2ETestContext>` field and `test_ctx()` method entirely
- Replaced ~15 `test_ctx().execute_op()` calls with `session().execute_operation().map(|_| ())`
- Replaced `test_ctx().query()` and `.query_and_watch()` calls with direct `engine().compile_to_sql()` + appropriate execute methods
- Updated `sut.rs`: removed `test_ctx()` calls (2 sites), routed `undo()/redo()` through `session()` instead of `engine()`
- Updated `polling.rs` helpers to take `&BackendEngine` instead of `&E2ETestContext`

**Decisions:**

- Session now owns the provider rather than storing it optionally — simplifies lifetime and guarantees DI services are available
- Kept `engine()` public and used directly for compilation/querying — `render_entity()`, `compile_to_sql()`, and `blocks()` genuinely need `BackendEngine` and don't fit the session abstraction

**Status:** All refactoring complete; `cargo build --workspace` and `cargo test -p holon-integration-tests --no-run` compile cleanly. No references to `test_ctx()`, `E2ETestContext`, or `from_engine()` remain in test_environment.rs.

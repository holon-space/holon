## Fixed SplitBlock whitespace divergence in org parser roundtrip

A property-based test (`general_e2e_pbt_sql_only`) was failing on `SplitBlock` operations due to interior whitespace normalization differences between the reference state and the org parser.

**What happened:**

- Identified that the org parser at `parser.rs:272` uses `headline.title_raw().trim()` (both ends), but two paired normalizers only called `trim_end()`
- Reproduced the bug: content like `" s  uhjdo"` (with leading space from regex generation) survived in reference and SQL states but was trimmed to `"s  uhjdo"` after an org write→parse roundtrip
- This caused subsequent `SplitBlock` operations to split different strings on the two sides, creating divergence
- Updated both normalizers to match parser behavior:
  - `normalize_content_for_org_roundtrip` in `crates/holon-integration-tests/src/pbt/types.rs:149`
  - `SqlOperationProvider::trimmed_content` in `crates/holon/src/core/sql_operation_provider.rs:195`
- Applied surgical fix: single-line branch now does `trim_start` after `trim_end`; multi-line branch uses `first.trim()` instead of `first.trim_end()`, preserving source-block carve-out
- Verified the persisted regression seed now passes (553s runtime)

**Status:** Fix landed and test passing. The normalizers now align with parser whitespace handling on both leading and trailing boundaries.

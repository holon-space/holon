## Hostile Code Review & Plan Refinement: Peaceful Strolling Muffin

Conducted a senior-dev code review of the streaming-reactive-widgets plan, treating it as a document full of implicit assumptions and potential landmines.

**What happened**

- Validated five critical factual claims against the codebase; found one outright wrong (`eval_to_value` already handles `FunctionCall`, not falling through to `Null`)
- Discovered naming collision: proposed `snapshot()` trait method conflicts with existing `ReactiveQueryResults::snapshot()` method
- Confirmed `row`/`col`/`section` builders panic on `CollectionData::Streaming`—plan didn't account for making them streaming-capable
- Verified holon-api already carries rhai/tokio/futures dependencies; the "minimal-deps contract" was overstated
- Identified non-trivial blast radius: `eval_to_value`/`resolve_args` imported across holon-frontend and multiple builders
- Cross-referenced with sister plan (`on-mobile-devices-android-transient-finch.md`), which *explicitly rejects* per-arg data-source approach

**Decisions**

- Keep composable, user-customizable widget approach (not a mega `focus_chain_bar`)—aligns with original intent
- Add `rows: HashMap<String, Arc<dyn ReactiveRowProvider>>` field to `ResolvedArgs` to minimize macro blast radius
- Replace static `widget_names` list with permissive Rhai resolver to eliminate two-list maintenance hazard
- Rename collision-prone `snapshot()` to `rows_snapshot`/`keyed_rows_signal_vec`

**Status**

Plan v3 rewritten with factual corrections baked in, design gaps filled (cache shape with blake3 fingerprint, 10-commit sequencing), and five open verification gates tracked as tasks. Ready for implementation kickoff.
